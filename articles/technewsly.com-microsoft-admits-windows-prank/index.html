Microsoft Admits Windows Was Just an Elaborate Prank

April Fool's Day came a few days late for Windows users this year. In a shocking announcement, Microsoft has admitted that its flagship operating system, Windows, was just an elaborate prank that has been going on for over 30 years.

"We wanted to see how far we could take this joke," said Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella in a press conference. "And honestly, we never thought it would last this long."

According to Nadella, the prank started in the early 1980s when Microsoft was first developing its operating system. A group of developers thought it would be funny to create an operating system that was intentionally difficult to use and filled with confusing menus and options.

"We never intended for anyone to actually use it," Nadella continued. "But then people started buying it, and we thought, 'Oh no, what have we done?'"

Despite its initial intentions, Windows quickly became one of the most widely used operating systems in the world, with over a billion users to date. And while many users have struggled with its confusing menus and often-frustrating updates, some have come to love the operating system for its quirks and unique features.

"I can't believe it was all just a joke," said longtime Windows user and tech enthusiast John Smith. "But I have to say, I'm kind of impressed that they were able to keep it going for so long."

While some users are understandably upset about the revelation, others are taking it in stride, seeing it as a lighthearted prank that they were unwittingly a part of. And while Microsoft has promised to release a new, easier-to-use operating system in the coming months, some users say they'll miss the quirks and frustrations that have come to define the Windows experience.

As for the future of Microsoft, Nadella says the company will continue to develop innovative products, but promises they won't be playing any more elaborate pranks on their users.

"We've learned our lesson," he said. "From now on, we'll be focusing on creating products that are actually useful."
